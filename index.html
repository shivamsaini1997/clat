Know Your CLAT Exam

The Common Legal Admission Test (CLAT), which is administered by the National Law Universities of India, is an all-India entrance exam for undergraduate and graduate legal programs. The exam is conducted for admission to integrated LLB (5 years) and LLM (1 year) programs.

Eligibility

Candidates who have passed their Class 12th with a minimum of 45% marks for the general category (40% for SC/ST/OBC/SAP) from a recognized board are eligible to appear for CLAT. Presently no upper age limit.

Application Process

The application form for CLAT can be filled by visiting the official website of NLU Consortium. Candidates can fill out the form by providing details such as personal, academic and contact information.

Exam Pattern

CLAT is a two-hour online test which consists of 150 multiple-choice questions from five sections: English, General Knowledge & Current Affairs, Elementary Mathematics, Legal Aptitude, & Logical Reasoning.

Syllabus

The syllabus for CLAT covers topics from English, General Knowledge & Current Affairs, Logical Reasoning, Legal Aptitude, and Elementary Mathematics. The syllabus is based on Class 10th and 12th.

Admit Card

The CLAT admit card can be downloaded from the NLU Consortium's official website. By entering the registration number and password, candidates can download their admit cards.

Result

The result of CLAT will be declared on the official website of NLU Consortium. The result will be in the form of a merit list that will indicate the name and roll number of the selected candidates.
